About Me

Hello! I'm Brett. I teach cello in Olathe and play in ensembles and other gigs around the KC and Lawrence area. Music can be a deeply personal and uniquely gratifying endeavor, and my goal is to help each student shape their own musical identity regardless of age or skill level. I've taught students as young as 7 years old, all the way up to recent retirees looking to finally scratch their musical itch. I teach mostly “classical” music but also dabble in pop, contemporary/modern, and have recently started studying jazz and improv. Regardless of goals I love to watch students improve, gain confidence, and develop their own personal meaning for why they play!

I've been playing cello for almost 20 years and have taught privately for more than 10. I have a Master's of Music degree in Cello Performance from the University of Nebraska, and Bachelor's degrees in Music Education and Cello Performance from Washburn University. Prior to teaching in Olathe I taught middle and high school orchestra in Manhattan, Kansas.

I offer cello lessons of 30 or 60 minutes at my residence. Students will have a weekly lesson at the same time/day each week. Students are charged a flat monthly rate based on lesson times:

In addition to lessons students can take part in studio recitals and other playing and performance opportunities. Interested students can take a free 30 minute “trial lesson” to see what lessons are like before making a time/financial commitment.

Upon first contacting me I will provide more info regarding scheduling, payment, attendance, performance opportunities, policies etc.
